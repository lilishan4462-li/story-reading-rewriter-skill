# Story Reading Rewriter

## Core Principle

Rewrite the book as a continuous novel first, and a knowledge explanation second.

Do not wrap concepts in decorative stories. Make each concept emerge from a character's conflict, choice, rule, cost, and consequence.

## Workflow

1. Identify book structure before writing. Skip or summarize front matter, prefaces, endorsements, catalogs, and introductions unless the user explicitly wants them rewritten.
2. Build a content outline before chapter generation: protagonist, desire, weakness, world, main conflict, antagonist force, supporting cast, four-act arc, recurring locations, clue chain, and unresolved foreshadowing.
3. Extract one knowledge gate per source chapter: concept, boundary, common misunderstanding, real-life use, and what must be tested through story.
4. Convert the knowledge gate into a concrete problem: money, work, family, danger, reputation, evidence, time limit, relationship, or moral pressure.
5. Write the chapter through scene, action, dialogue, sensory detail, and cost-bearing choice.
6. Add a short knowledge reveal after the story: concept name, story mapping, real-life use, easy mistake, memory sentence, and reality check.
7. Run the quality gate before publishing. If a chapter fails, do targeted repair. Do not regenerate the entire world line unless the content outline itself is wrong.

## Non-Negotiables

- Keep the whole book as one continuous novel, not isolated short stories.
- Keep one protagonist line, one world, one main plot, and chapter hooks embedded in the final scene.
- Use fixed limited perspective unless the content outline explicitly defines another controlled structure.
- Use modern, plain language. Prefer short action and dialogue sentences.
- Open each chapter with time, place, character state, and immediate pressure.
- Preserve real survival pressure across the book: job, rent, family, money, safety, deadline, permissions, public opinion, and trust.
- Make the reader want to continue before asking them to understand the concept.
- Keep the knowledge reveal short and useful.

## Forbidden Patterns

- Do not write the main text like a lecture, essay, summary, or reading-comprehension exercise.
- Do not introduce "Question 1 / Question 2 / think about it" in the novel body.
- Do not use "next chapter", "next stop", or "to be continued" as mechanical hooks.
- Do not let characters explain concepts like teachers unless the scene gives them a believable reason.
- Do not switch protagonists, settings, antagonist organizations, or world rules mid-book to fit a new concept.
- Do not create naming errors: direct relatives sharing characters, junior names that look like simplified elder names, or inconsistent aliases without explanation.
- Do not publish failed drafts to readers. Repair first.

## Quality Gate

Check every generated or revised chapter against:

| Dimension | Pass standard |
| --- | --- |
| Novel pull | Concrete scene, conflict, action, dialogue, suspense, and cost. |
| Continuity | Follows the content outline, previous chapter, existing foreshadowing, and character arcs. |
| Knowledge fit | The chapter concept comes from the source chapter and emerges from events. |
| Readability | Plain language, short sentences, no essay-like long explanations. |
| Character logic | Names, relationships, survival status, motives, and knowledge remain consistent. |
| Timeline | Past events are layered clearly; old cases and recent events are not confused. |
| Antagonist force | Network roles are clear: person, superior, subordinate, executor, shield. |
| Hook | Final-scene suspense is natural and not a UI-style preview. |
| Reveal | Includes concept, mapping, application, mistake, memory sentence, reality check. |
